Assessment Development

Assessment & Item Writing

Assessment questions written by someone who has done the work test the right things. Stems are grounded in real operational scenarios, distractors reflect actual misconceptions rather than arbitrary wrong answers, and rationale is written to teach — not just to confirm the correct response.

Work spans original item writing, full test bank development, exam review and difficulty rebalancing, and technical accuracy checks on existing content. Multiple-choice, short-answer, matching, and performance-based formats. Delivered formatted to your specifications, ready for instructor or platform use.

  • Original item writing — questions, distractors, and detailed answer rationale
  • Exam review and difficulty calibration
  • Full test bank development, end to end
  • Technical accuracy review of existing assessments
  • Illustration and explanation development for technical concepts
  • Performance-based and scenario-grounded item formats

Curriculum

Curriculum Design & Program Review

Curriculum that closes the gap between classroom and shop floor. Most industrial training programs cover the right topics in the wrong sequence, or cover the standard accurately but miss how the work actually happens. A practitioner review identifies both — and produces a roadmap to close them.

Work includes curriculum alignment to AWS SENSE, NCCER, and applicable industry standards, program audits for CTE and community college welding and fabrication tracks, sequence and coverage gap analysis, and advisory support for programs building new curriculum from scratch.

  • Curriculum alignment to AWS SENSE, NCCER, and OSHA standards
  • Coverage gap analysis with prioritized findings
  • Program audit for CTE and vocational welding tracks
  • Sequence and instructional flow review
  • Advisory support for new program development
  • Technical content review — textbook chapters, eLearning scripts, training materials
Research

Research Assistance

Specialized consulting for student researchers, faculty projects, and grant-funded programs where fabrication, materials science, or industrial safety expertise is part of the work. The value is a practitioner who has worked the material or the process — not just read the literature — and can help frame, validate, or pressure-test the technical dimensions of a project.

Engagements vary widely: technical advisory for a thesis project, subject matter input for a funded research program, review of technical methodology in a fabrication or materials context, or ongoing consultation over the course of a grant cycle. Open to both student researchers and institutional programs.

  • Technical advisory for thesis and graduate research projects
  • Subject matter consultation for grant-funded programs
  • Fabrication and materials methodology review
  • Industrial safety and regulatory research support
  • Practitioner perspective for academic or applied research contexts
